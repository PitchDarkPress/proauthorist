The person and the practice

About

Kevin Martin — writer, publisher, Proauthorist

Kevin Martin is a writer and the founder of Pitch Dark Press — an independent imprint that operates by a simple, uncompromising creed: Publish and Be Damned.

His writing spans multiple genres: comedy adventure, literary fiction, historical fiction, dark literary fiction. All of it shares a preoccupation with the moment when the ordinary world shifts — when characters are forced to discover who they really are underneath who they think they are.

He has been writing daily since 1986. He has been writing with AI since early 2025. He does not consider these two facts to be in conflict.

How the collaboration works

The Proauthorist process is a genuine creative partnership. Kevin brings the vision, the voice, the architecture, the characters, and the creative direction. AI brings ideas, challenge, research, and drafting capability.

The conversation between human and AI in this process is real. AI pushes back. It raises possibilities that weren't in the original plan. It takes material in unexpected directions that sometimes turn out to be exactly right. The work that emerges from that conversation is richer for it — and it could not have been made the same way by either party alone.

For Kevin, who has dyslexia, the collaboration carries an additional dimension. AI removes the friction that dyslexia places between thought and page — the stumbling over spelling, the uncertainty about grammar, the reliance on others for tasks that should be invisible. The creative mind works freely. The work is better for it.

"It is not press button, get book. It is a complete collaboration. Bottom line — it sets me free."

Pitch Dark Press

All of Kevin's published work appears under the Pitch Dark Press imprint. Not all titles involve AI collaboration — some are entirely Kevin's own hand. Where AI has been part of the creative process, it is declared openly — on the cover and in the text, as a matter of principle. The method is always declared. Pitch Dark Press does not hide its methods. It never has.

The imprint operates with complete independence. No agents, no traditional publishers, no compromises on content or creative direction. Publish and Be Damned is his motto. It is also a statement of real intent.

The full catalog of Pitch Dark Press titles is on the Works page.

Why Proauthorism needed a name

The word was coined in February 2026, but the practice it names had been underway for over a year before that. What prompted the naming was a desire to formalise a process that was already working — to give it a name, a definition, and a place to stand.

The timing was not accidental. AI is being weaponised by bad actors flooding the market with hundreds of machine-generated titles that have no human creative vision behind them. The damage is real — POD publishers are being forced to reconsider how they operate, and some are looking at charging for services that were once free, simply to deter the scammers. The legitimate indie author is paying the price for an abuse they had no part in.

Proauthorism draws a clear line between that exploitation and genuine human-led creative collaboration. They are not the same thing. They should never be spoken of in the same breath.

"Proauthorism is the name for using AI openly, honestly, and well — and for refusing to be confused with those who don't."
