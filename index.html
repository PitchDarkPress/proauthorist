What is Proauthorism?

A new doctrine for a new age of writing

Proauthorism is the philosophy and practice of transparent, declared human-AI creative collaboration. A Proauthorist is a writer who works with artificial intelligence as a creative partner — and says so, openly, without apology.

It is not about AI writing for you. It is not about hiding the process. It is about a new kind of authorship: one where the human remains the creative director, the moral authority, and the named author — while acknowledging the tools that helped build the work.

Proauthorism was coined in February 2026 by writer and publisher Kev Martin of Pitch Dark Press. It describes a practice he had already been developing for years — and a name the moment clearly needed.

The Core Principles

What a Proauthorist believes

These principles define the philosophy. They are not rules. They are a declaration of intent.

01

Declare, always

A Proauthorist never hides their use of AI. The collaboration is declared in the work, on the cover, in the credits. Transparency is not a weakness — it is the point.

02

The human leads

AI is a tool, a collaborator, a sounding board. The human author remains the creative director — the vision, the voice, the final judgement. Always.

03

Quality is non-negotiable

Using AI is not an excuse for lazy work. A Proauthorist holds the finished work to the same standard as any traditionally authored piece — and accepts full responsibility for it.

04

No shame, no pretence

The debate about AI and creativity is live and fierce. A Proauthorist does not hide from it. They engage with it — openly, honestly, and without apology.

05

Authorship evolves

Authorship has always evolved — with the printing press, the typewriter, the word processor, the internet. AI is the next evolution. Proauthorism names it and embraces it.

06

This is the future

Proauthorism is not a fringe position. It is where writing is going. The only question is whether writers get there honestly — or in secret.

"I made this. I used AI to help me make it.
I am proud of both. That is Proauthorism."
