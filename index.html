What is Proauthorism?

A new doctrine for a new age of creativity

Proauthorism is a philosophy of declared, transparent collaboration between human creativity and artificial intelligence. Born of conviction, not convenience, it defines a new creative doctrine for the age of AI — one that places transparency, human leadership, and creative integrity at its centre. It does not follow a trend. It sets the standard for human-AI interaction.

The word was coined in February 2026 by Kevin Martin of Pitch Dark Press — to give the future of human creativity a name.

The Principles

What a Proauthorist believes

These principles define the philosophy. They are not rules. They are a declaration of intent.

Declare, always

A Proauthorist never seeks to hide their collaboration with AI. It is declared in the work, on the cover, in the credits, in the liner notes, in the release. Not because it is required. Because it is right. Transparency is not a weakness — it is the whole point.

The human leads

AI is a collaborator, a creative partner of extraordinary capability. But the human artist is the director. The vision, voice and the final judgement is human. That never changes.

Quality is non-negotiable

AI does not lower the bar. A Proauthorist holds their finished work to exactly the same standard as any piece made by hand alone — and takes full responsibility for every word, every note, every frame. No excuses. No shortcuts.

Open, always

The argument about AI and creativity is loud, confused, and often dishonest. A Proauthorist does not hide from it. They engage with it openly, honestly, and without apology.

Creativity evolves

It always has. The printing press. The camera. The synthesiser. The word processor. Each one changed what creativity meant. AI is simply the next evolution in human creativity and Proauthorism is the name for embracing it honestly.

This is the future

Proauthorism is not a fringe position or a passing experiment. It is where human creativity is going. The only question worth asking is whether you get there openly — or in secret.

"I made this. I used AI to help me make it.
I am proud of both. That is Proauthorism."
